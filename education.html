Education — from childhood to now

A short, clean record of my formal learning and how it led into a life of exploration and self-taught craft. You can see the years, institutions, and notes directly in this file.

Journey Highlights

2008-2011 — Early Childhood Education - Anganwadi

Education has always held the highest place in my family’s values. My parents have always believed that knowledge is the true foundation of life — and they’ve never hesitated to prioritize our learning above everything else.

When I was around three years old, they sent me to the nearby Anganwadi — just about fifty meters from our home. I still remember those early mornings when my mother and father would walk me there hand in hand.

The Anganwadi was beside the small Kali Mandir, barely thirty meters away from our house. It’s one of my earliest memories — though I don’t quite remember who else studied with me there, I clearly remember the warmth, care, and the quiet beginning of what would become my lifelong journey of learning.

2011-2019 — Primary School Education

As I grew a little older, my learning world expanded — and so did my routine. I began attending two schools at the same time. Once a week, usually on Monday or Tuesday, I would go to a nearby Government Prathmik Vidyalay. The rest of the week, I studied at a private school — SSP School.

From Class 1 to 8, these two places became two sides of my early learning journey — one simple and traditional, the other more structured and academic.

During this phase, I met some truly special people who left a mark on my life.

In Class 5, I met my good friend Nitish, someone who made school days brighter and filled them with laughter and curiosity.
And in Class 6, I met Riya — a person I’ll always be grateful for. She taught me how to see the world with beauty and simplicity, not just through books but through perspective. Her kindness and grace still inspire me today.

Those years made learning feel interesting and alive. My parents constantly reminded me of the importance of education, and their words shaped how I looked at school — not as an obligation, but as an opportunity to grow every single day.

2019-2021 — High School / Secondary Education

After completing my primary education, I continued my studies in my own village — a place that always kept me close to my roots.

I joined Jairampur High School, a government school under the Bihar School Examination Board (BSEB).

Those two years — Class 9 and 10 — were full of growth, discovery, and confidence. High school brought new experiences, new friends, and a deeper connection with learning.

Among all subjects, Mathematics became my favorite. I enjoyed solving problems, exploring patterns, and finding beauty in numbers — it made me feel focused, challenged, and excited at the same time.

My high school days were not just about academics; they also taught me discipline, patience, and the value of consistent effort. Living in my village during this time gave me balance — simplicity at home and curiosity in school.

I scored 86.8% in my 12th Examination.

2021-2023 — Higher Secondary / Intermediate Education

I continued my studies in my village itself — completing Class 11 and 12 from Jairampur High School, under the Bihar School Examination Board (BSEB).

These two years quietly changed the way I looked at the world — and at myself. I began to notice things more deeply: the people around me, their struggles, their ways of thinking. Somewhere within, a shift started happening.

I began to question, to explore, and to understand what truly mattered to me.

I’ve always been a sensitive and curious person, eager to see every detail, to know the “why” behind things. During this time, that curiosity turned inward — I started searching for myself. While most people around me followed the same line, I somehow felt drawn to a different path, one that was uniquely mine.

After about two years, I met Riya again. She seemed a bit changed — and perhaps, so was I. That meeting made me realize that life keeps moving, people evolve, and it’s up to us to keep growing — for ourselves and for those we care about.

My friend Nitish was still with me, though our ways of seeing the world had started to differ a little.

When I entered Class 12, I grew frustrated with the local teaching methods — they no longer inspired me. So, I stopped relying on classroom lessons and began self-studying, observing life, society, and the world more closely instead.

That phase taught me independence and self-discipline. When the results came, I scored 76.4% in my 12th exams — a mark that meant more than numbers; it reflected my journey of understanding, effort, and quiet growth.

2023-2027 — Higher Eduaction / College

After completing my higher secondary education [2021-2023], I continued my studies and came to Delhi NCR . I joined Meerut Institute of Technology [2023-2027], a private college under the Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam Technical University, Lucknow [ AKTU ].
“Education is a process of changing ourselves, and everything related to us. — Nittyshry Prabhu”
